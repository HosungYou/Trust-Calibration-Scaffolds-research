# Trust Calibration Readiness: Terminological Defense and Positioning Analysis

**Date:** 2026-03-07
**Context:** Multi-agent analysis (Diverga A3, A2, B4, F4) + iterative discussion with author

---

## 1. Core Problem

"Calibration" across all disciplines = accuracy metric (correspondence between subjective judgment and objective outcome).
TCRS measures readiness/dispositions, NOT accuracy.
"Calibration readiness" has virtually no precedent (1 paper in retail training, different context).

## 2. Key Defense Arguments

### A. Alexander (2013) "Preparedness" Definition
- Alexander defines calibration as "the degree to which individuals' judgments about their understanding, capability, competence, or **preparedness** correspond to what they actually manifest"
- "Preparedness" = "readiness" — TCRS measures readiness, which is within Alexander's calibration scope
- Alexander's two foundational questions: Q1 (nature/bases of judgments) = what TCRS addresses

### B. Self-Report Is Intrinsic to Calibration
- Traditional calibration: self-reported confidence + objective performance → compute accuracy
- Confidence ratings ARE self-report — calibration research has always relied on self-report
- TCRS measures upstream process that produces quality of those self-reported judgments
- Not a departure from calibration tradition but an extension of it

### C. Dual-Referent Structure (Calibration ≠ Metacognitive Awareness)
- Metacognitive awareness = self-referential (awareness of own cognition)
- Calibration = relational (correspondence between internal state + external referent)
- Every TCRS subscale references BOTH trust (subjective) AND AI reliability (objective):
  - CA-Aw: trust awareness **relative to** AI reliability
  - CA-Jd: evaluation of trust–reliability **gap**
  - CA-Ac: behavioral adjustment **toward** trust–reliability alignment
- Trust calibration space (Figure 1) provides normative criterion (y=x line) absent from metacognition

### D. Calibration Accuracy as Predictive Validity Criterion
- TCRS measures readiness (stages 1-3 of process model)
- Calibration accuracy (stage 4: trust = reliability) = what TCRS should predict
- Empirical validation: do TCRS scores predict actual trust-reliability correspondence?

## 3. Bol et al. / Alexander (2013) Comparison

- **Bol & Hacker (2012)**: Calibration = accuracy of metacognitive judgments. Six research directions — none about "readiness"
- **Alexander (2013)**: Broader framing — includes preparedness, connects to self-regulation, self-efficacy, strategic processing
- **TCRS positioning**: Addresses Alexander's Q1 (bases of judgments), extends Bol's accuracy tradition to the dispositional level

## 4. Alternative Terms Considered

| Term | Advantage | Disadvantage | Decision |
|------|-----------|--------------|----------|
| Trust Regulation Readiness | Safe, connects to SRL | Loses calibration specificity | Rejected |
| Metacognitive Trust Monitoring | Precise for CA-Aw | Too narrow for CA-Ac | Rejected |
| Trust Self-Regulation Readiness | Full SRL connection | Gets buried in SRL literature | Rejected |
| **Trust Calibration Readiness** | **Novel, cross-disciplinary, dual-referent** | **Needs strong defense** | **Adopted** |
