## 4. EFA Prognosis After Revision

### 4.1 Improved Boundary Clarity

| Boundary | Before Revision | After Revision | Key Change |
|----------|----------------|----------------|------------|
| Aw vs. Jd | 55-65% separation | **70-80% separation** | Aw items now purely monitoring; Jd items use evaluative process verbs without self-efficacy |
| Jd vs. Ac | 65-75% separation | **70-80% separation** | Jd06 revised; Jd07/Jd12 softened; response format difference reinforces |
| Aw vs. Ac | 80-90% separation | **80-90% separation** | Already distinct; maintained |
| **3-factor overall** | **50-60%** | **65-75%** | Improved through linguistic sharpening |

### 4.2 Most Likely EFA Outcomes

1. **Three-factor solution (55-60% probability):** Clean separation as theorized. Most likely if items load as intended.
2. **Two-factor solution (30-35% probability):** Aw + Jd merge into "Calibration Cognition," Ac separate as "Calibration Action." Still publishable and theoretically coherent.
3. **Other (10% probability):** Method factors or poor fit requiring major revision.

### 4.3 Fallback Plans

| EFA Result | Interpretation | Paper Strategy |
|------------|---------------|----------------|
| 3 factors | Process model confirmed | Report as primary finding |
| 2 factors (Cognition + Action) | Awareness-Judgment distinction empirically inseparable | Report 2-subscale model; discuss Aw-Jd as theoretically distinct but empirically unified |
| 4+ factors | Subfacets emerge as factors | Consider bifactor model; may indicate facet-level rather than subscale-level structure |

## 5. Expert Panel (CVI) Preparation

### 5.1 Expert Panel Composition (Target: 5-7 experts)

| # | Expertise Area | Why Needed |
|---|---------------|-----------|
| 1-2 | AI in education / Educational technology | Content domain expertise |
| 1 | Trust in automation / Human-AI interaction | Trust construct expertise |
| 1 | Psychometrics / Scale development | Measurement methodology expertise |
| 1 | Metacognition / Self-regulated learning | Theoretical framework expertise |
| 1 | AI literacy | Related construct expertise |

### 5.2 CVI Rating Task

Each expert rates each item on a 4-point scale:
- 1 = Not relevant
- 2 = Somewhat relevant (needs major revision)
- 3 = Quite relevant (needs minor revision)
- 4 = Highly relevant

**Retention criteria:**
- I-CVI ≥ .78 (item-level content validity index)
- S-CVI/Ave ≥ .90 (scale-level average)
- Items with I-CVI .60-.77: discuss with panel, revise or drop

### 5.3 CVI Rating Form Structure

Experts receive:
1. **Construct definitions** for each subscale (with process model explanation)
2. **Item pool** with subscale assignment
3. **Rating form**: relevance (1-4) + open comment field per item
4. **Cross-classification task**: "Which subscale does this item best fit?" (to test intended assignment)
