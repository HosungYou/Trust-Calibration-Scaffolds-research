## Research Orientation Domain Analysis

**Domain coding scheme:** Each of the 97 coded papers was assigned to one of five research orientation domains (D1–D5) based on its primary intellectual contribution and framing.

- **D1 — Technology Adoption / AI Acceptance:** Studies primarily framing trust as a predictor or outcome of AI adoption (TAM/UTAUT paradigm)
- **D2 — AI in Education (AIED) / Learning Outcomes:** Studies focusing on AI's effects on learning, performance, or pedagogy where trust is secondary
- **D3 — AI Literacy / Critical AI Competence:** Studies examining students' ability to critically evaluate, understand, or interact with AI systems
- **D4 — Trust Awareness in Educational AI:** Studies foregrounding trust as a central educational concern, with three sub-orientations (see §D4 below)
- **D5 — Trust Calibration Research:** Studies explicitly measuring or theorizing calibration accuracy — whether trust matches actual AI reliability

#### Key observation: the disciplinary blind spot

The composition of D5 provides the clearest quantitative evidence of the disciplinary blind spot this manuscript addresses. Of the 81 papers retrieved through systematic database searches of educational AI literature, only 3 (3.7%) reached the threshold for D5 classification. By contrast, 13 of 16 supplementary papers (81.3%) — drawn from automation trust, human factors, cognitive science, and metacognition literatures — qualified as D5.

This pattern indicates that trust calibration as a rigorous construct is **not absent from the literature broadly**, but is **largely invisible within the educational AI research community's primary publication venues and search terms**. The 13 supplementary papers exist in adjacent disciplines and have not been systematically integrated into educational AI trust discourse — a structural disciplinary gap rather than a general research gap.
